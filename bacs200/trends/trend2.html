The Popularization of Investing


The investment world used to be very different before the development of worldwide networking through the Internet, and the services that have grown since then. One example of this growing technological accessibility is the existence of investment apps, with the most popular example being Robinhood. With investment opportunities and capabilities sitting at the tips of our fingers, investing has become a popular option for the common man. This has led to a more "gamey" perspective of investment strategies, where the one and only goal is to make money the fastest by any means necessary, rather than strategically investing in businesses with high prospects. The biggest example of this in recent memory is the GameStop (GME) stock incident. This incident involved members of the r/WallStreetBets subreddit (a subreddit is a community of users with one similar interest) banding together to artificially inflate the value of GameStop's stock, with the intention being to make large amounts of money by selling the stock after it grows as well as intending to screw over stock shorters. Shorting a stock involves a lender lending a stock to a short seller, who expects that stock to decrease in value. The short seller then immediately sells the stock for a lump sum of money, and then buys back the stock after the price drops in order to return the stocks to the lender. The users of r/WallStreetBets see this as unethical gambling, and wanted to make all the short sellers of the GameStop stock to have to pay much more than the original price that they sold for, and because they are legally obligated to give the stocks back, they must buy the stocks back, even if they are much more expensive than before. This forced buying back of the stocks initiates a short squeeze, where the price increases further because of the larger amounts of transactions being performed with the stock.

Another example of modern investment trends comes with the advent of cryptocurrency and non-fungible tokens (NFTs). Cryptocurrencies are unregulated digital currencies that are made possible using intensive hashing software. The real-world value of cryptocurrencies fluctuates in the same way that stocks and similar investments do, so cryptocurrencies are treated as investments more than as actual currency. NFTs fall under a similar category, as they are digital items that are minted with a unique identifier than can be exchanged, that is a "proof-of-ownership," despite not actually acting as legal ownership at the time of writing. These NFTs tend to be treated as investments, although that would be putting it generously. They are more often traded within a small group of people to artificially increase the value until a genuine buyer believes that the NFt holds value, and buys it for an exhorbitant price, even though the actual value of the NFT is likely much lower. These communities that "own" these NFTs have been growing, and despite being very controversial, seems to be getting some attention from serious investors.
