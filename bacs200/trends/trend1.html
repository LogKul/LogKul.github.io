Social Media And Shrinking Attention Spans






Important Modern Trends


According to Facebook, the average attention span was just a measly 2 seconds for people on Facebook viewing video content, with desktop users having an average attention span of 2.5 seconds while mobile users average to 1.7 seconds. This is incredibly unhealthy, and the result of poor self-control of users who spend a lot of time on social media. Because of this, I believe that gluttonous social media consumption is harmful for our behavior regulation and will lead to negative neurological trends if we do not learn to regulate our internet activity. Failing to regulate instantly-gratifying behaviors such as scrolling through endless feeds of information can lead to dopamine addictions, where brain chemistry is altered to seek out quicker and easier rewards in response to environmental stimuli. Normally, human activity is regulated through the brain's reward system, which makes us feel good for accomplishing something, whether it be big or small, such as getting errands done or completing a stressful exam. However, with constant access to endless feeds of gratifying information, the effects of accomplishments feel miniscule in comparison to the amount of feel-good content that is available by scrolling a social media feed. Unfortunately, regulation does not seem to be on the horizon, and I worry about the long-term consequences for humanity that will result from this blatant abuse of our brain chemistry.
